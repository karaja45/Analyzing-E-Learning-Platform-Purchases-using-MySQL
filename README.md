# Analyzing E-Learning Platform Purchases using MySQL

**Descriptive Analysis**

* Total purchases and revenue are unevenly distributed across learners and courses.
* A few courses account for most enrollments.
* Certain categories generate higher revenue due to higher prices, not higher volumes.
* Some learners have purchased from multiple categories, showing broader engagement.
* A few courses have no purchases at all.

**Diagnostic Analysis**
* High revenue from specific courses is high.
* Learners purchasing across categories are Career-focused on Upskilling.
* Country-wise spending differences may be caused by regional demand for certain skills.

**Predictive Analysis**
* Popular courses will continue to dominate enrollments unless new alternatives are introduced.
* Learners who have already purchased multiple courses are more likely to buy again and spend higher amounts in the future.
* Categories with high revenue, but fewer learners may grow slower in volume but remain profitable.

**Prescriptive Analysis**
* Focus on marketing campaigns on top-performing courses.
* Offer course bundles for learners buying across categories.
* Apply dynamic pricing or discounts for low-performing courses.
* Improve, rebrand, or discontinue courses with zero enrollments.
* Use country-specific offers to maximize regional revenue.
* Introduce loyalty programs for repeat learners.

**Conclusion**

The analysis shows that revenue is driven by a small group of high-performing courses and highly engaged learners. Strategic focus on top categories, cross-category learners, and optimization of underperforming courses can significantly improve revenue and learner retention. Data-driven decisions will enable sustainable growth for the e-learning platform.
